First, it is important to note that **humans will survive**, the question is how many humans, where, and at what cost. Actions against climate change can be justified considering only human situation, including animals and environment only makes the required action stronger.


## Effects
Exact effects depend on how much the Earth warms. But what can be certain right now is increased sea levels that will lead to many people being displaced, regions becoming to hot or humid to live in (including possibly densely populated India). If the warming reaches 5 or 6 degrees Celsius, mass extinction becomes serious risk.

Many problems for human health (like Malaria) will also become more prevalent, but their prevention is most likely cheaper directly rather than via climate change. However warmer climate reduces deaths due to cold. Seems like there is no consensus.

Directly observable effect is number of expensive natural disasters in the world.

Even without climate change, pollution itself significantly reduces lifespan and life quality.

## Cost of Inaction vs Action
Some articles indicate that inaction will be much much worse than action, but verify to ensure up to date info and proper research.

It is however difficult to put exact price tag on action and inaction, some things are just dependent on moral values - like value of driving animal to extinction or forcing people to migrate. 

One thing worth noting is that, regardless of climate change, [[Renewable_Energy|Renewable Energy]] is already cheaper than fossil fuels and it is sustainable well into the future, compared to fossil fuels which are predicted to run out in this or next century.

In some ways, action **directly** reduces costs, by for example removing fossil fuel subsidies, which are still very prevalent. 

Costs are distributed very unevenly, inaction would be mostly paid by already poor countries.
