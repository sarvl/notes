#science 

Heat pumps are essentially [[Air_Conditioner|Air Conditioner]] running in reverse.

A refrigerator needs to overcome the process of [[Entropy]]. A heat pump is used to displace heat from one spot to another, even from lower temperature one to higher. They generally work by using refrigerants which can have their [[Matter_State|Boiling Point]] easily modified by varying [[Pressure]]. The process of changing phases takes a lot of energy, the energy which does not affect the temperature of the substance. By forcing refrigerant to vaporize or condense, energy can be transformed from one place to another.

Compressor is used to force refrigerant in particular direction  and to rise its pressure from being low density gas. With very high pressure its boiling point is raised well above ambient temperature, it also gets hot. Then it passes through condenser which makes it cool down and turn back into liquid, transferring heat from it to environment. Most of this heat comes from evaporator where the pressure is very low (via use of some device restricting flow), this low pressure makes boiling point below ambient temperature, boiling requires taking energy, after it cools down due to boiling, it gets heated up by the environment.

This makes heat pumps at least as efficient as resistive heating, usually much better. Turning gas into electricity and then using heat pumps is more efficient than burning gas on site.

One way to allow heat pumps to work in cold climates is via ground heat pumps, since the ground below some point never freezes.
