#math 

Mathematics is organized deductive reasoning used to solve a certain problem. Often this reasoning is in the form of a [[Proof]].
A Conjecture is a [[Logic#Statement|Statement]] thought to be true without a proof, a proven Conjecture is a Theorem.

Mathematics is a skillful operations with concepts and rules invented just for this purpose. Invention of new concepts is important as this allows us to, retroactively, create axioms, otherwise we would quickly run out of interesting things to do.

In a way, mathematics is just art which happens to have surprisingly many real world applications, but most people who do practice math do it because of the art part.

## History
Mathematics started as a formalized way to deal with real world entities, particularly regarding [[Geometry]]. 

## Interconnectedness
Many math concepts appear in seemingly unrelated fields, consider for example $\pi$ defined as ratio of circumference of a circle to its diameter, it appears in [[Probability#DistributionNormal|Normal Distribution]].
 
## Application in Natural Sciences 
Despite its abstract definition and concepts, mathematics is surprisingly useful in natural sciences - especially [[Physics]]. We do not really understand why, but its precision, accuracy, and commonality in doing so suggest it is not just an accident and it is not just not our bias towards it. Moreover, laws formulated in language of mathematics often predict more than we put in them - like predicting gravitational waves by [[General_Relativity|General Relativity]].

[[Complex_Number|Complex Numbers]] are a great example of this, nothing in our experience could possibly suggest that $\sqrt{-1}$ actually exists, yet it not only exists, it is central component in [[Quantum_Mechanics|Quantum Mechanics]], mainly due to its appearance in [[Wave_Mechanics|Wave Mechanics]] from [[Differential_Equation|Differential Equations]].

## Education
The state of math education is unfortunately poor if not nonexistent, most of the curriculum is about *notation* and *arithmetic* and not about actually *doing* any sort of *math*.  A great  analogy is set up by Paul Lockhart - imagine music education where the focus is on standard notation used by musicians instead of actually playing any instruments or learning any songs. 

Forcing student to memorize formulas and rules is not bad by itself - it is even often necessary to be proficient at mathematics - but when it is the problem when it becomes the core of curriculum.

School education also lacks criticizing proofs - their clarity and their correctness. Too often theorems are shown as divine truth.

Overfocusing on practical aspect is counterproductive, fantasy by itself is interesting to people.

Significant part of the problem is teachers who are neither actively creating mathematics nor even know what mathematics really is.
