#philosophy #science

Our theories are limited by the choice of *data* and *observations* we use to test these theories. It is entirely feasible that if we picked different points of interest, we would create entirely different theories that still make complete sense. In fact, this is current the state of our knowledge - [[Quantum_Mechanics|Quantum Mechanics]] and [[General_Relativity|General Relativity]] are two mutually excluding theories, both of which are perfectly reasonable in their own domains, and we have not yet found any exception to them.  


## Research
The notes here are quite general and not applicable to every field all the time.
Note that most published research is not even significant, most research is slowly pushing the field forward.

### Doing

### Verifying/Reading
- During the first read or scan of the paper, decide whether it is in any way relevant, whether it is introducing something new to the pool of knowledge. This new thing can be actually new research or it can be simply synthesizing existing knowledge in particularly good way. 

### Refereeing 
- During the first read or scan of the paper, decide whether it is in any way relevant, whether it is introducing something new to the pool of knowledge. This new thing can be actually new research or it can be simply synthesizing existing knowledge in particularly good way. 
- Distinguish between badly written paper and paper with no contribution, both shouldn't be published but the former can be improved.
- Report should contain: 
	1. Brief recommendation and reasons for it;
	2. Summary of point of the paper;
	3. Evaluation of the validity and significance of the research;
	4. Evaluation of the quality of the paper;
	5. Overall recommendation.
- Do not waste time reading deeply flawed papers.
- Evaluate paper based on:
	1. Its purpose;
	2. Its fitting for the journal/field.
	3. Significance of its goal.
	4. Validity of its approach.
	5. Correctness of the methods.
	6. Correctness of the conclusions.
	7. Quality of presentation.
	8. New information.
