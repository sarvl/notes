#science 

Solar roadways are implementation of [[Solar_Panel|Solar Panels]] as smart roads that works neither as solar panels nor roads.

Smart roads are a bad idea to visibility, roads are created specifically such that all markings are well visible in daylight and during the night. [[Diode|LEDs]] are not very well visible during the day from different angles. 

All the electronics, mesh, and networking incredibly rises price and complexity of road system.

Assuming $185W/m^2$ peak [[Sun]] power. With $20\%$ loss in surface area, $10\%$ loss in inverters and cables, $10\%$ loss in perfect glass, $20\%$ loss due to flat angle, leads to $90W/m^2$.  Over the day with $7\times$ multiplier, and over year of $0.6$ rated output, leads to $380Wh/m^2$ a day. About $24\%$ will be consumed by LEDs, on bad day (and each night)  the  road has to be supplied with power. 

A lot better solution is to simply install solar farms.

No real world experiment  proved them as viable technology, while some did generate power, all of them were significantly more expensive than even rooftop installation, let alone solar farms. This includes the solar roads without any markings.
Even cycle paths are not realizable and degrade quickly.
