#electronics 

Any device dissipates [[Heat]], so thermal management is needed to ensure the device operates in its designed [[Temperature]] range. More specifically, proper temperature of [[Semiconductor|Semiconductor Junction]] has to be ensured.

## Theory
Thermal design is completely equivalent to [[Circuit_Design|Circuit Design]] in concepts.
[[Current]] corresponds to [[Power]], [[Resistance]] corresponds to Thermal Resistance, [[Voltage]] corresponds to Temperature. [[Capacitance]] corresponds to Thermal Inertia.

![[Thermal_Design_Theory.png]]
By assuming certain power is generated by junction, and simply solving finding temperature drop at each thermal resistor, one can find the resulting junction temperature.


## Heatsink
Generally, heatsink datasheets contains thermal resistance assuming the [[Heat]] is evenly put into the heatsink, this is never the case. As a rule of thumb, increase it by $33\%$ to get heat from point load.
Also keep in mind that Air Flow affects the temperature significantly
